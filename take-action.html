Why Your Donation Matters

The situation in Yemen is currently the worst humanitarian crisis on earth. In 2019, Yemen experienced one of the world’s worst food crises, and it is expected to remain one of the most severe. Access to food is so limited that approximately 17 million people don’t have enough to eat, and there is a heightened risk of famine due to prolonged and severe food access constraints of the collapsing economy and COVID‑19. Below are several organizations you can donate to in order to help those in Yemen!

Where You Can Donate

The United Nations Foundation works toward maintaining and continuing advancements in society. Each month, the UN’s World Food Programme (WFP) distributes 100,000 metric tons of food commodities at more than 5,000 points, reaching more than 8 million Yemenis. The World Health Organization (WHO) and the UN Children’s Fund (UNICEF) have reached more than 300,000 people with cholera vaccines. To donate click here!

The United Nation's World Food Programme fights the world's worst hunger crisis around the globe. They have provided over 12 million monthly food assistance in Yemen. They are also providing cash assistance to help inject money into the local economy. WFP is providing nutritional support to 1.1 million pregnant and nursing women and children under 5.To donate click here!

Mercy Corps is an organization that provides humanitarian aid across the globe. Just in 2017 Mercy Corps provided 3.7 people in Yemen with food assistance, clean water, and more. They supply clean water to more than 20,000 people in Yemen each day and are currently providing food assistance to more than 350,000 people in the country. To donate click here!

Islamic Relief USA is a leading humanitarian organization in Yemen, with more than 3,000 field staff and volunteers in the country. Since the most-recent conflict began in 2015, IRUSA has been delivering life-saving aid in almost all Yemeni provinces, including dangerous areas like Ta’iz, Saa’da, and Aden. IRUSA has served more than 580,000 people in Yemen and have conducted 25 projects in the country as well. To donate click here!
